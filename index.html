Meet The Animals Of Jim Corbett National Park

Jim Corbett National Park, India’s first national park, is a sanctuary of natural beauty and diverse wildlife. Established in 1936 as Hailey National Park and later renamed in honor of the legendary hunter-turned-conservationist Jim Corbett, it spans over 520 square kilometers in the Nainital district of Uttarakhand. The park’s landscape is a rich tapestry of hills, riverine belts, marshy depressions, and grasslands, with an elevation ranging from 1,300 to 4,000 feet. It’s a haven for over 110 tree species, 50 species of mammals, 580 bird species, and 25 reptile species. The park’s dense moist deciduous forests are dominated by sal trees, and it’s renowned for its Bengal tigers, which thrive under the Project Tiger initiative. As an ecotourism destination, Jim Corbett National Park is a testament to the beauty and resilience of India’s natural heritage. Jim Corbett National Park, nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas in Uttarakhand, India, is a treasure trove of wildlife and natural beauty. It covers an area of 520.8 square kilometers and is celebrated as the first national park established in India. The park was initially named Hailey National Park in 1936, during the British Raj, after William Malcolm Hailey, a governor of the United Provinces. In 1956, it was renamed in honor of Jim Corbett, the renowned hunter and naturalist who played a pivotal role in its creation.

Jim Corbett Zone's for Tourism

Bijrani Safari Zone

The Bijrani Safari Zone in Jim Corbett National Park is a realm where the wild resonates with a symphony of nature’s most profound tunes. This zone, known for its lush sal forests and expansive grasslands, offers an unparalleled safari experience. As you embark on a journey through Bijrani, you’re greeted by the vibrant hues of flora that are a visual treat.The area is a mosaic of diverse habitats, from dense woodlands to serene water streams.

Jhirna Safari Zone

The Jhirna Safari Zone of Jim Corbett National Park is a testament to the transformative power of conservation. Once a farming village, Jhirna was redeveloped into a thriving habitat and added to the park in 19941. Situated at the southern edge of the reserve, it’s a landscape reborn, offering visitors year-round access to its dry deciduous forests and large grasslands known as Laldhang Chaur.

Know More

Dhela Safari Zone

The Dhela Safari Zone, a relatively new addition to Jim Corbett National Park, is a beacon of eco-tourism and wildlife conservation. Sprawling over 1,173 hectares, it was inaugurated in 2014 as the sixth zone of the reserve. This zone is a mosaic of mixed forest plantations and moist deciduous forests, providing a sanctuary for a diverse range of fauna, including the majestic Bengal tiger.

Know More

Durga Devi Zone

The Durga Devi Zone of Jim Corbett National Park is a sanctuary where the wild’s untamed beauty is on full display. Located on the northeastern periphery of the park, this zone is a haven for those seeking an immersive experience in the lap of nature. The zone is distinguished by its hilly terrain and dense forests, crisscrossed by the meandering Ramganga and Mandal rivers.

Know More

Dhikala Zone

The Dhikala Safari Zone is the crown jewel of Jim Corbett National Park, offering an immersive experience into the wild. It is the largest and most varied zone, presenting an unparalleled vista of the valley with the Kanda ridge in the backdrop. This region is rich in wildlife and is famous for its sprawling grasslands, dense sal forests, and numerous water bodies.

Know More

Sitabani Buffer Zone

The Sitabani Safari Zone, though not a part of the core Corbett Tiger Reserve, is an enchanting buffer zone that offers a serene and rich wildlife experience. Known for its lush forests and ancient temples, Sitabani holds a special place in the hearts of nature enthusiasts and mythological history buffs alike. It is believed to be the site where Goddess Sita, from the epic Ramayana, spent her days of exile.

Know More

Garjiya Safari Zone

The Garjiya Safari Zone, also known as the Ringora Safari Zone, is a captivating addition to the Jim Corbett National Park’s array of wildlife experiences. This zone, situated at a convenient distance from Ramnagar, has quickly become a favorite for wildlife enthusiasts. It is particularly renowned for its picturesque landscape and tranquil environment, which is highly admired by visitors.

Know More

Phato Safari Zone

The Phato Safari Zone is a captivating addition to the Jim Corbett National Park, offering a unique blend of adventure and natural beauty. Inaugurated in 2022, this zone is a part of the Terai West Forest and is adjacent to the Amangarh Tiger Reserve. Covering an area of 2516.70 hectares, the Phato Zone is a sanctuary for a diverse array of wildlife, including the majestic Royal Bengal Tiger, various deer species, and an elephant corridor.

How To Reach Jim Corbett Safari Zone

There are many ways to travel to Jim Corbett as all the means of reaching there have been discussed below:

By Road – The way to Jim Corbett by road from Delhi is via Noida, Kashipur, and then finally Ramnagar. It takes 7-8 hours from Delhi to reach Corbett national park from Delhi via road.

By Train – Ramnagar is the closest railway station and is well connected to some of the major places like Delhi, Varanasi, Haridwar, and Lucknow. However, people travelling from other parts of India, will have to first come to Delhi and then take a connecting train to Ramnagar.

By Bus – Again Ramnagar is the popular destination as buses from places like Delhi, Haridwar, Dehradun and other places. One can take a bus to Ramnagar and from there it’s quite easy to reach the camping site of Corbett National Park.

By Air – This in a way is not an idea way to get here. However, there’s a daily flight fron Delhi to Pantnagar and from there the approx distance to Jim Corbett National Park is 80 odd kilometres. One can take a cab and reach there in around two hours.

Jim Corbett Safari Booking Procedure:

Determine which safari zone you would like to visit (Dhikala, Bijrani, Jhirna, Durga Devi, Dhela) and the corresponding entry gate. Each zone offers a unique wildlife experience.

There are multiple ways to book a Jim Corbett Jeep Safari:
  • Online Booking: Visit the official website of the Corbett Tiger Reserve or authorized booking platforms to check availability and make reservations.
  • Authorized Agents: You can also book through authorized travel agents or tour operators who facilitate safari bookings.

Decide on the type of safari you wish to book. The options typically include:
  • Morning Safari: Early morning safaris offer a chance to witness wildlife in their natural habitat during the prime hours of activity.
  • Afternoon Safari: Afternoon safaris provide a different perspective of the park’s wildlife as they wind down their day.

Provide necessary details such as the number of participants, identification proof, preferred safari date, and zone preferences.

Check for availability of safari slots on your preferred date and zone. Popular zones or peak seasons may have limited availability, so it’s advisable to book well in advance.

Once you find an available slot that suits your preferences, proceed to make the payment for the safari. Online platforms usually allow for secure payment methods.

After successful payment, you will receive a confirmation of your safari booking, including details such as the safari date, time, entry gate, and other relevant information.

On the day of the safari, arrive at the designated reporting time and entry gate. It’s advisable to reach a bit early to complete any required formalities.

Carry a valid photo ID for verification purposes at the entry gate.

Once your booking is verified, you’ll be assigned to a designated jeep and guide. Listen to the guidelines provided by the park authorities and your guide for a safe and enjoyable safari experience.
